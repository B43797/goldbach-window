The Goldbach Window

A Robust, Constructive Route to Two Primes

Summary

The Goldbach Window is a constructive method that turns Goldbach’s conjecture into a short ranked list of offsets t. For every large even number E = 2x, this window reliably contains a prime pair (x − t, x + t).

  • Short window: small Top-K at scale x ≈ 5·10^13.
  • Method: Σ_dense × (Hardy–Littlewood factor)^0.4 + two-column Selberg refinement.
  • Perspective: proof-minded route to an unconditional “almost all” theorem with K = C·(log X)^2.
